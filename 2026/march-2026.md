# 4 March

How would people grasp the depth of a person's transformation if they secluded themselves away from prying eyes?

How would people be blessed if one sought the crowd's acceptance more than their own authenticity, for fear of exclusion?

I was that person.

Prim, polished, angelic, brainy, close to perfect—that's how I wanted people to envision me when I was younger. However, my internal world was anything but.

Lost, chaotic, dark, tangled, and tortured—this is what a person would have encountered behind my polished facade. Never-ending mental torture, held together by a slick ponytail, a fresh, powdered face, and a sweet smile, with a "good reputation" being the thread that pieced it all together.

And no one would have believed a word I confessed. They would have thrown their usual accusations at me, asserting that I was merely whiny and ungrateful. They would say, "You could never feel that; you are smart and capable! You should see my pain; it is much worse than yours."

Maybe.

Maybe not.

You haven't sacrificed what I sacrificed. It was unwilling at first, but it later became what was expected.

You just see the glitz and the glamour: the highs, the awards. You will never know what it cost me to have those.

I miss my mom. I miss my dad. How I long to have had a childhood with my sister. How I wish I had grown up in my hometown.

If only I could choose growth and comfort together; but alas, one cannot in this world. The harsh reality is that you must give up one to have the other.

I don't know how much longer I have to endure. But I will. I must. I shall see it through to the end.
