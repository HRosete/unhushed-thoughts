# 13 December

While I was gazing at my calculations, I realized how I admire the strokes I wrote in confidence more than those I timidly scribbled out of doubt and fear. I reckon that's how it is with life as well: strut with confidence and boldly take up your own space.

But it doesn't work as easily as I thought it should. How can you be confident if you anchor your being to something as superficial as the acceptance and applause of people who are as unstable as you are? Or cloud your mind with the illusion of elitism on the grounds of wealth and knowledge, which ebb and flow according to the stock market and time-appropriate societal norms? How pitiful are we, then, if we don't have anything unchanging, timeless, and stable to steadily anchor our whole being and identity? I can then compare us to chaff in the wind: it goes where the wind blows, unaware and worryful of its fate, just accepting the fact that it is what it is. How sorrowful that life must be.

Thank goodness I have been found and rescued by someone who is as firm as a rock and as wise as the depths of the sea. He clung to me tightly so I would not be tossed to and fro by the raging waves and roaring winds. He gave me the safety I have been unconsciously yearning for; He embraced me lovingly as a good father to his young daughter.

Although I initially thought of Him as strict, unyielding, and brutal when He plucked out the thorns in my skin, the dagger in my heart, the black hole in my core, the ants in my mind, and the chains strapped to my body—His meticulous act of wellbeing-surgery saved me from the deadly path I and my predecessors' decisions paved for me, into a journey of light, healing, love, and firm, absolute truth. This, I might say, is the road less traveled.
